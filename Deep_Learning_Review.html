Deep Learning Fundamentals Lab Review

Deep Learning Fundamentals Lab Admissions Quiz (Part 1)

1. What is the identity matrix?
Answer: A square matrix with 1s on the diagonal and 0s elsewhere.

2. Why is matrix multiplication not commutative in general?
Answer: AB ≠ BA because order affects the computation.

3. Find the determinant of [[3, 2], [1, 4]].
Answer: 10. The determinant is calculated as (3 * 4) - (2 * 1) = 12 - 2 = 10.

4. What is the null space of a matrix?
Answer: All vectors x where Ax = 0.

5. How are the rank and nullity of a matrix related?
Answer: Rank + Nullity = number of columns. This is known as the Rank-Nullity Theorem.

6. Evaluate: "Orthogonal matrices preserve lengths and angles." Is this true?
Answer: True. They represent rotations and reflections, which are transformations that preserve both length and angles.

Deep Learning Fundamentals Lab Admissions Quiz (Part 2)

7. Why is f(x) = |x| not differentiable at x = 0?
Answer: Left and right derivatives are different.

8. What is the gradient of a scalar function?
Answer: A vector of all partial derivatives.

9. Calculate lim(x->0) sin(x)/x.
Answer: 1.

10. Find the derivative of f(x) = sin(2x).
Answer: 2cos(2x).

11. How do partial derivatives relate to directional derivatives?
Answer: The directional derivative is the dot product of the gradient with the direction vector.

12. Create a function with exactly two critical points.
Answer: f(x) = x³ - 3x.

13. What's the difference between discrete and continuous distributions?
Answer: A discrete distribution has countable outcomes, while a continuous distribution has outcomes that fall within intervals.

Deep Learning Fundamentals Lab Admissions Quiz (Part 3)

14. What is a random variable?
Answer: A variable whose values depend on chance.

15. What type of distribution models coin flips?
Answer: Binomial.

16. If you flip a fair coin 3 times, what's P(exactly 2 heads)?
Answer: 3/8.

17. How does the Central Limit Theorem work?
Answer: The distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size n increases.

18. Create a scenario where mean and median are equal.
Answer: Any symmetric distribution.

19. Why are Python lists different from arrays in C?
Answer: Lists can hold mixed types and resize dynamically.

20. What is the value of 2 ** 3?
Answer: 8.

Deep Learning Fundamentals Lab Admissions Quiz (Part 4)

21. How do you check if 5 is in list [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]?
Answer: 5 in [1, 2, 3, 4, 5].

22. Why does "2" + 3 raise an error?
Answer: Python doesn't implicitly convert types.

23. How does Python's garbage collection work?
Answer: Automatic reference counting.

24. Evaluate: "Lambda functions can replace all def functions." True or false?
Answer: False. Lambdas are limited to expressions.

Q 1.0.2.1: How does changing β affect the graph of a linear function y=β+ωx?
Answer: It shifts the line vertically.
